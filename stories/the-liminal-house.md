# I

The house had been waiting. Eleanor knew this the way one knows a dream is a dream — not through logic, but through the texture of the air itself.

She stood at the gate, suitcase in hand, watching the windows catch the last of the November light. The estate agent had described it as *characterful*. That was one word for it.

The key turned with a sound like a held breath being released.

> “Every house has a memory,” her grandmother used to say. “The good ones forget. The bad ones don’t.”

Inside, the hallway stretched ahead — longer, she thought, than the exterior suggested. The wallpaper carried a faded pattern of leaves and antlers. At the far end stood a mirror draped in a grey sheet.

She closed the door behind her and the whole place seemed to settle into attention.
