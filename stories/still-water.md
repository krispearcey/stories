# I

No one in the village could explain why the reservoir never rippled.

Mara first noticed it at noon, when the trees around the banks leaned under a hard western wind and the water remained perfectly level, black-blue and reflective as polished stone.

She crouched at the edge and touched the surface.

It was warmer than her hand.
