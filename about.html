About

Kris Pearcey writes literary horror and contemporary weird fiction. His stories have appeared in a handful of journals and anthologies, typically the ones with unsettling cover art and editors who describe themselves as “interested in the liminal.”

He lives in the English countryside, in a house that is — as far as he can tell — exactly the size it appears to be from outside. He writes in a room overlooking a garden that behaves itself most of the time.

His fiction is interested in memory, landscape, old rooms, failed certainty, and the quiet ways the ordinary begins to tilt. He is especially drawn to stories where dread arrives gradually and refuses to explain itself.

When he is not writing, he is usually reading, revising, or convincing himself that a sound in another room was only the house settling.
