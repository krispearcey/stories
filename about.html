About

Kristopher crafts literary horror rooted in the rugged, atmospheric landscapes of Atlantic Canada. His work explores the intersection of the natural world and the inexplicable, often finding dread in the quiet corners of the suburbs or the deep, dark bogs of Newfoundland.

His storytelling is driven by atmospheric tension and the distortion of the familiar. He focuses on the moments where the environment itself feels occupied and the ordinary world begins to shift into something unrecognizable.

When he isn’t writing about things that shouldn't be in the water or lurking in the woods, he balances a dozen different obsessions. He is a front-end developer by trade, an avid fly fisher, a chess enthusiast, and a lifelong fan of the Montreal Canadiens. Whether he’s hip deep in a gully or playing sports, he’s always thinking up new ways to make a reader look twice at a shadow.
